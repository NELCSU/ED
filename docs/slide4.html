Statistical Inference Model

Dummy data was generated to represent four scenarios where the location is either UCC or ED and the streamer role is nurse or GP.

Data was simulated for each site, spanning 400 days. For each day, two metrics were derived:

  1. Proportion of patients streamed either to ED or UCC (ED/UCC)
  2. Proportion of patients returning to the UEC system after completed episode (% Return)

Visualising these two metrics in two dimensions allow us to locate clusters. These clusters illustrate the streamer type by location interactions.

If we can determine that the variation with clusters is less than the variation between clusters, we're well-placed to infer significance.

Peaks drawn in either axis give us the projection in either dimension (the ED/UCC streamed to ratio, and the % returning to UEC services); and these can be used to demonstrate significance in either dimension.
